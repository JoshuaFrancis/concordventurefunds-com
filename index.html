Investment thesis

Three sectors where Concord Venture Capital concentrates its conviction.

01

B2B software

Vertical SaaS and horizontal software platforms with $1M to $10M in ARR, net revenue retention above 110%, and a sales motion that scales without proportional headcount growth. Particular interest in regulated industries where software reduces compliance burden, automates manual workflows, and creates meaningful switching costs for buyers.

02

Deep technology

Companies with proprietary technology at their core — materials science, photonics, advanced manufacturing, quantum computing, and biotechnology platforms with multiple commercial application pathways. Typically pre-revenue or early-revenue at entry, with IP moats strong enough to withstand a 5-to-10 year commercialisation horizon.

03

Fintech and insurtech

Financial services infrastructure companies, embedded finance platforms, and insurance technology businesses that serve underserved customer segments or replace legacy system costs for incumbent institutions. Canadian regulatory expertise is a meaningful differentiator in cross-border expansion into US and EU financial markets.

Who this domain fits

For venture managers who want a brand founders trust and LPs recognise.

Emerging fund managers

First and second-time venture fund managers raising institutional capital who need a domain that communicates seriousness and scale before the track record exists to speak for itself. Concord Venture Capital reads as a firm, not a side project, which changes how LPs and founders respond to the first outreach email.

Corporate venture arms

Large Canadian companies establishing a strategic investment vehicle to maintain exposure to early-stage innovation without running it through a corporate legal department. A standalone domain creates the separation from the parent brand that founders prefer when deciding whether to take strategic capital alongside institutional money.

Accelerator alumni funds

Accelerator programme graduates and angel syndicate leaders formalising an investment vehicle after deploying capital informally.
